Custom Raytracing Projects

This project showcases a series of raytracers I built from scratch during my academic journey. I implemented both CPU-based and GPU-accelerated versions to simulate realistic lighting, shadows, motion blur, reflections, and materials. The project taught me key graphics concepts like ray-sphere intersection, diffractions, recursion for reflections, and pixel shaders. In other projects I also experimented with OpenGL where I made rasterizations instead of ray tracing. This taught me about manipulating projection matrices, using multivariable calculus, and learning about how computer graphics work.
